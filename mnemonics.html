⚠️ Critical Engine Inoperative Vmc Factors (CRMALOFT)+

C – Critical Engine Inoperative (failed engine windmilling, creating high drag)

R – Rearward Center of Gravity (reduces rudder effectiveness, increases Vmc)

M – Most Unfavorable Weight (lighter weight can increase Vmc)

A – Airborne / Out of Ground Effect (higher Vmc due to accelerated slipstream)

L – Landing Gear Up (removes drag on failed side, allows faster yaw)

O – Operating Engine at Max Power (maximum yaw on good engine)

F – Flaps Up (flaps up allows higher Vmc)

T – Trimmed for Takeoff (rudder trim set for takeoff increases Vmc)

🛩️ Critical Engine Determination (PAST)+

P – P-Factor (Asymmetric Thrust — descending blade produces more thrust; left engine's thrust arm farther from CG)

A – Accelerated Slipstream (Roll — right engine creates accelerated airflow over wing; more lift farther from CG causes severe roll when left engine fails)

S – Spiraling Slipstream (Yaw — left engine slipstream strikes vertical stab from left, helping counteract yaw from failed right engine)

T – Torque (Roll — clockwise torque from right engine causes greater roll toward dead engine in left engine failure)

🔄 Pre-Maneuver Checklist (CCMALS)+

C – Clearing Turns (visually checking for traffic)

C – Configuration (setting aircraft — flaps, power)

M – Minimum Safe Altitude (1,500' or 2,000' AGL)

A – Airspeed (establishing proper speed)

L – Landing Area (identifying emergency landing spot)

S – Scan/Seatbelts (scanning for traffic and securing belts)
