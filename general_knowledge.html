GENERAL KNOWLEDGE

📋 Documents & Currency+

👨‍✈️ Required Pilot Documents

• Valid Government Issued Photo I.D.

• Current Pilot's Certificate

• Current Medical Certificate

• Logbook (student pilots only)

⏰ Pilot Currency Requirements

General: Flight Review, FAA Checkride, or WINGS program within preceding 24 calendar months

With PAX: 3 T/O and LDGs within preceding 90 days (must be full stop if night or tailwheel)

IFR Flight: 6 Instrument Approaches, Holding, Intercepting, and Tracking Course within preceding 6 months (or IPC)

🏥 Medical Certificates

1st Class: Valid 12 calendar months (6 months if over 40)

2nd Class: Valid 12 calendar months

3rd Class: Valid 60 calendar months (24 months if over 40)

*1st reverts to 2nd which reverts to 3rd class privileges

🗺️ Airspace & Weather Minimums+

🗺️ Airspace Dimensions

Class A: FL180 to FL600, entire U.S., extends 12 NM off coastline

Class B: Surface to 10,000 MSL in multiple shelves. Typically extends to 30 NM.

Class C: Surface to 4,000 AGL with 1 shelf (lower shelf starts at 1,200 AGL). Typically extends to 10 NM.

Class D: Surface to 2,500 AGL. Typically extends to 4 NM.

Class E: From SFC, 700 AGL, or 1,200 AGL up to 17,999 MSL.

Class G: Surface to 700 AGL or 1,200 AGL. Max 14,500 MSL (out west)

*Hierarchy: A > B > C > D > E > G

📡 Airspace Equipment Requirements

Class A: IFR flight plan, IFR equipment, IFR current

Class B: Two-way radio, Mode C transponder, clearance into Class B

Class C: Two-way radio, Mode C transponder, establish 2-way contact

Class D: Two-way radio. Establish 2-way radio contact

Class E & G: None

☁️ VFR Weather Minimums

Class A: VFR N/A

Class B: 3SM, Clear of clouds

Class C & D: 3SM, 1000 above / 500 below / 2000 horizontal (3-152)

Class E below 10k: 3SM, 1000/500/2000 (3-152)

Class E above 10k: 5SM, 1000/1000/1SM (5-111)

Class G <1200 AGL day: 1SM, Clear of clouds

Class G >1200 AGL, <10k: 1SM, 1000/500/2000 (3-152)

Class G >1200 AGL, >10k: 5SM, 1000/1000/1SM (5-111)

Class G night: 3SM, 1000/500/2000 (3-152)

⛰️ Minimum Safe Altitudes (91.119)

Anywhere: Altitude allowing safe landing if powerplant fails without undue harm to persons or property

Congested areas: 2000 ft horizontal, 1000 ft above highest obstacle

Other than congested: 500 ft above the surface

Over water/sparse areas: 500 ft from any person, vessel, vehicle, or structure

⚖️ Regulations & Procedures+

⚠️ Inoperative Equipment Test (91.213)

Step 1: VFR Day Type Certificate

Step 2: Kinds of Operations List

Step 3: 91.205 Requirements

Step 4: Airworthiness Directives

If item passes all 4 steps → deactivate and placard INOPERATIVE, or remove and redo W&B

*MEL items do NOT require the 4-step test. MELs are tail number specific.

🎛️ Instrument Cockpit Check

Airspeed: Reading 0 kts

Altimeter: +/- 75 ft of field elevation

Attitude: Erect, bank no more than 5°

Compass: Swinging freely, full of fluid, known headings

D.G.: Matches compass, moves correctly during turns

Turn and Bank: Ball to outside of turn, coordinator goes with turn

Vertical Speed: Reads 0 fpm

⚖️ Aircraft Load Factors

Normal: +3.8 g / -1.52 g

Utility: +4.4 g / -1.76 g

Acrobatic: +6.0 g / -3.00 g

🫁 Supplemental Oxygen (91.211)+

🫁 Oxygen Requirements by Altitude

12,500 MSL: Minimum flight crew must use supplemental oxygen for any period greater than 30 minutes

14,000 MSL: Minimum flight crew must use supplemental oxygen

15,000 MSL: All flight crew must use supplemental oxygen. Passengers must be supplied with supplemental oxygen.
